## Candidate Niches

### 1. **Family Care Coordinator**
- **Target audience**: Families managing a loved one's serious illness (cancer, chronic disease, elder care, post-surgery recovery)
- **Pain point**: Coordinating 10-30+ people around hospital visits, meals, donations, medication schedules, and doctor updates using scattered tools (shared Notes, group texts, spreadsheets)
- **Why underserved**: CaringBridge is a blog/journal, not a coordination tool. Lotsa Helping Hands exists but is dated and web-only. Meal Train only does meals. Nothing combines visit scheduling + meal coordination + donations + medication tracking + personalized summaries for different audiences (patient, close family, extended circle)
- **Opportunity signal**: ~2M new cancer diagnoses/year in the US alone. Elder care coordination is a growing need. Extremely emotional purchase = high willingness to pay. Strong word-of-mouth potential (families tell other families). Founder has deep personal experience.
- **Keywords**: care coordination app, family hospital visits, cancer caregiver app, caregiver scheduling, meal train alternative

### 2. **Smart Reminders / AI Task Assistant**
- **Target audience**: Busy adults (especially new parents, professionals) who use calendar apps as a dumping ground for reminders and tasks
- **Pain point**: Reminders just nag — they don't help you accomplish the task. No context, no preparation tips, no "did you think about X?" nudges. Calendar gets cluttered with non-events.
- **Why underserved**: Apple Reminders, Google Tasks, Todoist — all just list managers. None use AI to proactively help you prepare for or accomplish the task. The "smart" part (AI brainstorming tips, preparation checklists, contextual suggestions) doesn't exist yet.
- **Opportunity signal**: Reminders/to-do is a massive category but heavily commoditized at the basic level. AI differentiation could carve out a premium niche. "Busy parent" is an enormous, growing audience.
- **Keywords**: smart reminders app, AI task planner, AI to-do app, reminder assistant, task preparation app

### 3. **New Dad / First-Time Parent Companion**
- **Target audience**: First-time dads (and parents generally) overwhelmed by the transition to parenthood
- **Pain point**: Fragmented information across baby tracking apps, parenting forums, and Google searches. No single app that helps dads specifically navigate the emotional, logistical, and practical sides of new fatherhood.
- **Why underserved**: Most parenting apps are designed for moms (baby tracking, breastfeeding). Dad-specific apps are almost nonexistent. The "trying to conceive" and "new parent" communities are massive on Reddit.
- **Opportunity signal**: ~3.6M births/year in the US. Dad communities growing on Reddit and Discord. Founder is literally living this right now.
- **Keywords**: new dad app, first time father app, dad parenting guide, baby prep for dads, fatherhood app

### 4. **Busy Gamer Backlog Manager**
- **Target audience**: Adult gamers (25-45) who have growing backlogs and limited free time
- **Pain point**: Buying games faster than playing them. No good way to decide what to play next based on available time, mood, and what friends are playing. Existing backlog trackers are just lists.
- **Why underserved**: Backloggd, HowLongToBeat, and GG exist but are primarily databases/social networks, not decision-making tools. None answer "I have 45 minutes, what should I play?" intelligently.
- **Opportunity signal**: r/patientgamers has 900K+ members. "Backlog shame" is a meme across all gaming communities. Adult gamers are the highest-spending demographic.
- **Keywords**: game backlog tracker, what to play next app, gaming backlog manager, busy gamer app, game time optimizer

### 5. **Vendor / Purchase Experience Tracker**
- **Target audience**: Collectors and frequent online buyers (TCG, sneakers, vinyl, etc.) who buy from many different sellers/platforms
- **Pain point**: No way to track which vendors ship fast, pack well, offer deals, or are reliable. This info lives in notes apps or memory. TCGPlayer/eBay reviews don't capture your personal buying patterns.
- **Why underserved**: Marketplace reviews are per-transaction, not per-vendor-relationship. No tool aggregates your personal buying experience across platforms.
- **Opportunity signal**: Collectibles market is booming ($400B+). Power buyers in TCG, sneakers, and hobby communities are underserved. Niche but passionate = high willingness to pay.
- **Keywords**: vendor tracker app, seller rating tracker, collectibles purchase log, TCG buying tracker, multi-platform seller reviews

### 6. **Group Plans Coordinator**
- **Target audience**: Friend groups (20s-40s) trying to make plans together
- **Pain point**: Group texts devolve into chaos. Polling for dates/times is clunky. Nobody commits. Plans fall apart.
- **Why underserved**: Doodle is for meetings. Partiful is for events/parties. Nothing nails casual "let's hang out this weekend" coordination for adult friend groups.
- **Opportunity signal**: Huge TAM but a graveyard of failed attempts — suggests the UX challenge is hard but the need is real. Social/viral growth potential.
- **Keywords**: group plans app, friend hangout planner, social coordinator app, make plans with friends, group scheduling app
